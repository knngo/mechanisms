What is a Mechanism?

A mechanism is a complete process…a “virtuous cycle” that reinforces and improves itself as it operates.

A mechanism delivers ongoing outputs that address a business challenge. To deliver these outputs, a mechanism takes controllable inputs, and transforms them.

Characteristics of a Mechanism

Circumvents good intentions.
Iterates and improves over time.
Helps us get work done.
Is a complete, closed loop process.
Ensures that inputs lead to outputs.
Incorporates inspection to ensure the mechanism is effective.

When do we need Mechanisms?

We recognize that a process relies on good intentions.
We spot an incomplete process.
An error (or similar errors) keep occurring.

Why should I use a Mechanism?

As an organization grows, more of the work occurs outside of leaders’ direct line-of-sight. The number and complexity of decisions increases, as do the challenges of communicating and coordinating among the parts of the organization. As the amount of data and decisions that demand attention increases, attention itself becomes an ever more constrained resource.

Mechanisms enable leaders to manage their daily work and guide the organization beyond their direct line-of-sight and to allow their influence to persist over time.
